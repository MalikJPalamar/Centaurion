# The Markov Blanket

## Concept

In Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle, a **Markov Blanket** is the boundary that separates a system's internal states from external states. All information exchange between inside and outside passes through this blanket. It's what makes a system a *system* — distinguishable from its environment.

A cell membrane is a Markov Blanket. A skull is a Markov Blanket. The Centaurion exo-cortex has one too.

## The Daemon Agent IS the Blanket

The Daemon agent serves as the boundary manager:
- **Inward:** Ensures external information is properly contextualized before reaching internal states. Raw data → tagged Supermemory entries → synthesized wiki pages → Graphiti entities.
- **Outward:** Ensures internal decisions are properly expressed to the external world. Decisions → API calls → Telegram messages → GitHub commits → client deliverables.
- **Integrity:** The blanket's job is to maintain the distinction between self and environment. The exo-cortex knows what IT knows vs. what the WORLD states. It doesn't confuse its model of reality with reality itself.

## Why This Matters

### Without a Blanket
Information flows freely in and out. The system has no coherent identity. External prompts override internal values. The agent becomes a stateless tool — no memory, no direction, no compound learning.

### With a Blanket
Information flows through defined channels. Identity persists across sessions. External noise is filtered before reaching internal states. The system compounds: every interaction makes the internal model more accurate, which makes the blanket more effective at filtering.

### The Coupling Law Connection
Law 3 (the Coupling Law) requires shared model state between human and AI. The Markov Blanket ensures this sharing happens through proper channels, not ad hoc. Supermemory, wikis, and Graphiti are the defined channels. Everything else is noise.
