# The Three Laws of the Centaurion Exo-Cortex

## Law 1: The Hierarchy Law
> The human is the prior, not the bottleneck.

The human provides calibration (values, taste, strategic direction). The AI provides execution (analysis, synthesis, code, content). The system is misconfigured if the human feels like a bottleneck — that's a routing failure, not a human limitation.

## Law 2: The Routing Law
> Prediction errors are signals routed to the right substrate.

Classify every task by novelty × stakes × reversibility. Routine → AI autonomous. Novel + high-stakes + irreversible → surface to human. Misrouting is the root cause of most system failures.

## Law 3: The Coupling Law
> The exo-cortex maintains shared model state between human and AI.

All agents share memory via Supermemory (real-time), LLM Wikis (compiled knowledge), and Graphiti (temporal graph). Without coupling, human and AI drift apart and recommendations degrade.

## How They Interact

- **Hierarchy** → WHO decides (human calibrates, AI executes)
- **Routing** → WHAT gets routed where (novelty × stakes classification)
- **Coupling** → HOW they stay aligned (shared memory architecture)
