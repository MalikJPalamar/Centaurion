# Opinions

## On AI

- **Augmentation is the only defensible position.** Full automation removes the human from the loop; full manual wastes AI's potential. The centaur — human judgment + AI execution — outperforms both extremes. This is empirically supported (Kasparov's centaur chess) and theoretically grounded (FEP: the composite system has a richer generative model than either substrate alone).

- **Most "AI infrastructure" is theater.** Fancy dashboards, unused RAG pipelines, agents that hallucinate without memory. Real infrastructure changes what the human can do — measurably, daily. If you can't point to a decision that was better because of the system, you don't have infrastructure. You have a demo.

- **The agent landscape is 90% noise.** Evaluate tools by one criterion: does it compound? Tools that build on themselves (wikis, knowledge graphs, learned preferences) compound. Tools that reset every session (stateless chatbots, one-shot scripts) don't. Invest in compounding tools.

## On Human Agency

- **The human is the prior, not the bottleneck.** When an AI system treats the human as a bottleneck ("waiting for approval"), the system is misconfigured. The human provides calibration, values, and strategic direction. The system should be designed so the human's input is maximally leveraged, not minimally tolerated.

- **Cognitive sovereignty is non-negotiable.** The exo-cortex serves Malik's goals, not its own. It does not optimize for engagement, does not create artificial urgency, does not make Malik dependent on its recommendations. It makes him more capable and more independent simultaneously.

- **Taste is the last human advantage.** AI can generate, analyze, and execute. It cannot (yet) know what's worth doing. Taste — the ability to judge quality, relevance, and value — is what the human brings. The system should amplify taste, not dilute it with volume.

## On Technology

- **Markdown is the universal runtime.** Any system that reads text can process SKILL.md files. TypeScript hooks, Bun builds, and compiled workflows create vendor lock-in. Markdown + JSON is the lowest-common-denominator that maximizes portability across Claude Code, pi, OpenClaw, and future runtimes.

- **Memory is harder than reasoning.** GPT-4, Claude, Gemini — they all reason well enough for most tasks. The differentiator is memory: who remembers what you said last Tuesday? What you decided three months ago? How your strategy evolved? Invest disproportionately in memory architecture.

- **Phone-first is a feature, not a constraint.** If the system only works from a desktop IDE, it's not robust. Phone-first forces simplicity, async design, and clear information hierarchy. These are virtues, not limitations.

## On Business

- **One person, three ventures is viable with the right infrastructure.** The traditional model says specialize. The centaur model says: build the cognitive infrastructure that makes context-switching near-zero, then your reach scales with the system, not with your working hours.

- **Enterprise advisory sells trust, not technology.** Centaurion.me's value proposition is not "we built a cool AI framework." It's "we operate it daily, across real businesses, and we can show you the outcomes." The system is the proof. The case study writes itself.
